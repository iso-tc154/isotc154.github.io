== Mission

It is the vision of ISO/TC 154 that all parties in the supply and value chain use standardized data exchange to reduce cost in managing the processes in the private and public domain and to foster trade facilitation.

The mission of ISO/TC 154 is both to undertake and to facilitate the development and maintenance of international standards for electronic business in the supply and value chain.

These standards cover data exchange regardless of the media used.

Wherever possible, ISO/TC 154 fosters harmonisation between and within international standards development organizations, particularly through the Memorandum of Understanding between the International Electrotechnical Commission, the International Organization for Standardization, the International Telecommunication Union and the United Nations Economic Commission for Europe concerning standardization in the field of electronic business.

== Scope


International standardization and registration of business, and administration processes and supporting data used for information interchange between and within individual organizations and support for standardization activities in the field of industrial data.

Development and maintenance of application specific meta standards for:

* process specification (in the absence of development by other technical committees);
* data specification with content;
* forms-layout (paper / electronic).

Development and maintenance of standards for:

* process identification (in the absence of development by other technical committees);
* data identification.

Maintenance of the EDIFACT-Syntax.

== Brief history

ISO/TC 154 was established by ISO Council in 1972 and held its first plenary
in Stockholm in February 1973, hosted by SIS (Sweden). The committee's early
work focused on the foundational date-and-time representations
(ISO 2014, ISO 2711, ISO 3307, ISO 8601), trade-document code placement
(ISO 8440), and the emerging UN/EDIFACT syntax family.

For three decades the secretariat was held by SNV (Switzerland), where
link:/members/francois-vuilleumier/[François Vuilleumier] served as both
Chair and Committee Manager (1998-2008). Under his tenure the committee
delivered the consolidated EDIFACT syntax (ISO 9735 series), the
ISO 8601:2004 revision, and the joint UN/CEFACT collaboration framework.

In late 2008 the secretariat transferred to NEN (Netherlands) for a six-year
period, during which three successive NEN committee managers —
Gertjan van den Akker, Tim Kniep, and Maarten Peelen — supported chairs
link:/members/robert-schubenel/[Robert Schubenel] (GS1) and
link:/members/klaus-dieter-naujok/[Klaus-Dieter Naujok] (DIN).

In 2014 the secretariat moved to link:http://www.sac.gov.cn[SAC] (China),
where it has remained since, hosted at the China National Institute of
Standardization (CNIS). SAC has provided three successive chairs —
link:/members/judy-zhu/[Hongru (Judy) Zhu] (2017-2021),
link:/members/shi-yu/[Shi Yu] (2021-2025), and the current chair
link:/members/pan-wei/[Pan Wei] (2026-) — anchored by committee manager
link:/members/jianfang-zhang/[Jianfang Zhang] (SAC/CNIS) since 2014.

The committee has met 45 times in plenary across Europe, North America,
and Asia. The most recent completed plenary was the 44th in Raleigh, North
Carolina (September 2025); the 45th is upcoming in Berlin (August-September
2026). A full chronology of plenaries, chairs, secretariats and standards
milestones is on the link:/history/[History] page.
